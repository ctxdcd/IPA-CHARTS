Vowel family map

Short vowels as a family tree

A, I, U, E, O are the “head of the family.” In English, each one has different “children” based on duration, tongue position, and mouth shape.

Family A

A

Anak 1 · Si Lebar /æ/ — short, very open mouth. Example: cat
Anak 2 · Si Santai /ɑː/ — long, relaxed. Example: car
Anak 3 · Si Tajam /ʌ/ — short, central sound. Example: cup
Family I

I

Anak 1 · Si Cepat /ɪ/ — short, relaxed. Example: sit
Anak 2 · Si Panjang /iː/ — long, smiling. Example: seat
Family U

U

Anak 1 · Si Pendek /ʊ/ — quick and short. Example: foot
Anak 2 · Si Monyong /uː/ — long, rounded lips. Example: food
Family E

E

Anak 1 · Si Burung /ɜː/ — long, flat sound. Example: bird
Anak 2 · Si Pemalas /ə/ — very weak and unstressed. Example: teacher
Family O

O

Anak 1 · Si Kaget /ɒ/ — short, a little open. Example: hot
Anak 2 · Si Dalam /ɔː/ — long, deeper sound. Example: door
